How to work with Kanban in CRM

Author: Anna Molitvik
Last updated: November 3, 2025.

Kanban is a board with columns and cards that helps guide the client through the sales funnel. Cards are CRM elements: leads, deals, invoices. Columns are the stages of working with the client, in Bitrix24 they are called stages.

Kanban is available in leads, deals, invoices, proposals, and smart processes.

In the deals kanban, you can track their quantity and amount at each stage, control the workload of managers, and see which deals have not yet been taken into work.

Go to the section CRM, select the desired element and open Kanban.


How to set up kanban for deals

Let's consider the kanban settings using deals as an example.

Setting up stages. Stages are the steps in working with clients. With the help of stages, you can track the sales process from the first contact to the completion of the deal. Stages can be configured in kanban or in CRM directories.
How to set up stages in CRM directories

By default, there are several stages in deals. You can use them, modify, or create new ones.

  • The initial stage is the first step in working with the client; new deals fall into this stage. The initial stage cannot be deleted, but it can be renamed.
  • Additional stages include document preparation, sending invoices, signing contracts. You can create any number of stages.
  • Final stages are successful or unsuccessful deals. There can be several unsuccessful stages, which will help analyze the reasons for the refusal to purchase.

To change the name, color, or delete a stage, click on the Pencil. If you need to add a new stage, click on the Plus (+).

Setting up the deal creation card. Deals can be created automatically, for example, from an email from a client, or they can be created manually directly in kanban. To allow managers to quickly create a deal manually, select the fields that need to be filled in. For example, the name, client data, deal amount. The other fields can be filled in later.

Click on Settings (⚙️) > Kanban Settings > Configure Creation Card, select the necessary fields and save the changes.

To quickly create a deal, click on the Plus (+) at the desired stage. Fill in the fields and click Save.
How to create a deal

Setting up the deal view card. To ensure that not all fields are displayed in kanban, but only important information, configure the view card. Each employee can set it up for themselves. For example, it is important for the manager to see the client's name, the deal amount, and the payment status. The rest of the information can be viewed by opening the deal.

Click on Settings (⚙️) > Kanban Settings > Configure View Card, select the fields and save the changes.

How employee avatars are displayed in the deal card. In kanban, the avatar of the manager responsible for the deal is displayed by default. As work progresses with the deal, it may automatically change to the avatar of another employee if they:

  • left a comment in the timeline,
  • are responsible for an incoming or missed call, incoming email or chat,
  • are responsible for a task for which an automatic reminder was triggered. For example, 15 minutes before the deadline of the task, the avatar of the person responsible for it will be displayed in the deal card.

How to work with deals in kanban

Moving deals through stages. Move deals through stages as you work with the client. For example, when you take a deal into work, move it from the stage New Deal to the stage In Progress, and when you sell the product — to the stage Deal Successful.
To do this, grab the deal with the mouse and move it to another column.

Planning tasks. To avoid forgetting to contact the client, plan a task — a call, meeting, or contract preparation. To do this, click on + Task, fill in the card and click Save.
Universal task in CRM

In the deal card, there is a task counter. The number shows the number of incoming and planned tasks, and the color indicates deadlines. By default, the counter lights up red 15 minutes before the task deadline. This time can be changed.
How task counters work in CRM tasks

To view information about the task, click on the counter.

Contacting the client. To quickly view contacts, write or call immediately, hover over the client's name. A mini-CRM card with basic information will open: client type, company, phone, and email. From here, you can also go to the full card to edit the data.


In Brief

  • Kanban is a board with columns and cards that helps guide the client through the sales funnel.
  • Cards are CRM elements: leads, deals, invoices. Columns are the stages of working with the client, in Bitrix24 they are called stages.
  • In kanban, you can track the number and amount of deals at each stage, control the workload of managers, and see which deals have not yet been taken into work.
  • Kanban is available in leads, deals, invoices, proposals, and smart processes.
  • Set up stages in kanban and move cards through them until you complete the deal.
  • To allow employees to quickly create a deal in kanban, set up the creation card.
  • To ensure that not all fields are displayed in kanban, but only important information, set up the deal view card.
